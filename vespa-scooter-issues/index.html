NEW YORK – Piaggio Group Americas Inc. cancels certain 2020-2021 Vespa models for brake line problems that could reduce braking performance, according to documents from the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA).

The problem first came to light through warranty claims for excessive play in the front and rear brake levers.

Lambretta Versus Vespa: The Two Italian Scooter Tribes That Took Over The World

According to NHTSA Part 573 Safety Recall Report 20V-617, the cause was “improper galvanizing of the new zinc-nickel brake line brake pipe terminals. The protective coating of the -zinc/nickel used to treat the surface of the brake pipe terminals was found to have an improper coating process. This poor treatment can leave hydrogen on the surface during the treatment, which is released into the brake fluid, resulting in the release of hydrogen from the brake system.”

This faulty process allowed hydrogen to build up and then be released into the brake system, causing bubbles. Bubbles in the brake lines are not good. Increased brake lever travel during braking can increase stopping distance, which can also increase the risk of a crash.

Piaggio is addressing this issue by notifying owners and authorized dealers to perform a free O-ring test, change the brake fluid and bleed the brake system, and replace the lines of the brakes if necessary.
