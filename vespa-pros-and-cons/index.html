Vespa Pros And Cons – NOTE: This is my first post on the BHP Group. I will try to post my thread as simply as possible. I need the support of all respected members of this community to guide me not only in posting my shopping experience but also in the long term review of my two/four wheelers. I am proud to be a BHPian.

As a new member to this forum, I will certainly admit that reading and writing about cars is a very difficult task that not everyone can do. It’s like listening and understanding a language you don’t know. My love for cars started from my childhood when my Dad (deceased) used to own a Rajdoot 175. The sound of the double stroke engine and the smell of the 2T mixture & Gasoline smoke still burning at the end. my nose My interest in cars is not limited to two wheels but also four wheels. I will never forget the long and comfortable ride in the Hindustan Ambassador 1500 DSZ. As a 10-year-old child I silently marveled and embraced the beauty of the musical instrument placed right in the middle of the embassy where I lived. Besides those bikes have always been in my dreams since I finished my 10th standard. I don’t remember when I learned to ride a bike properly but yes, I tried it for the first time when I was 14 years old. My study room was full of great bike wall posters that I had bought at the market with savings from my pocket. Speaking of scooters, I’m not too keen on them. I hated them because I was afraid to ride one. The process begins with the complexity of looking at the motorcycle from one side, cranking the engine and leveling the entire scooter, pressing the clutch hard with the palm of your left hand and at the same time putting in the gear which is not my cup of tea. But there are many people in our village who love motorcycles more than bicycles. I still wonder why?

I bought my first bike in April 2008. A black Apache RTR 160. As it was the first product of its kind by TVS which used to be in the non-gear scooter segment. run away. The self starter itself was replaced 3 times in a short span of 15 K miles. Tired of the same problem over and over again I finally sold it in 2011 and bought a Bajaj Pulsar 180 UG 4. I loved the overall performance of the Pulsar which served me selflessly until 2019. I really wanted a quick upgrade. I ended up selling a pulsar to one of my coworkers with 88K miles on odo. Then it was time to choose another bike with high CC and power and when I closed my eyes I saw Dominar D 400. Already impressed with the reliability of Pulsar 180 I never thought of buying the newly launched D. 400. It has now covered more than 9K miles and I find the maintenance cost very cheap compared to the OTR price of the bike.

My Vespa Vx

Coming to the topic, in 2011 I also bought a TVS gogo 110. It is mainly used for grocery shopping and short rides across the city. My wife used to go to her office which is 13 km away from home. Over 44 K kilometers in Odo, Wego has served us happily for nine years. But what I don’t like is the quality of the plastic, the cold start problem and the braking problem. It also had an accident a few years ago after the chassis needed some work which I thought was inappropriate considering its age and mileage. I finally sold the staff to another company last month.

Finding the right location for TVS Wego was not an easy task for me. What I was looking for was a good scooter that would be stylish, strong, reliable and durable. After searching the internet for a while I have listed a few scooters that will meet some of my preferences. The price doesn’t really matter because I want to buy something that is reliable for a long time. I also don’t want to compromise on ride quality. As my wife is going to use it sometimes I wanted it to be a little thinner and heavier. I took my search to quick results and decided to visit some dealers located in Bhubaneswar City.

B) The street price is close to 97 K. The sales person wants me to book it on the same day and he promised me about other accessories for free!
