After a month or two, I decided to give myself an early Christmas present and bought a 2008 Vespa PX125. This time, I did my research and knew with 100 percent certainty that I could not only convert to a EV, but also ships it in the UK without too much hassle.

The Vespa PX series was built from 1977 to 2017, with a difference in emissions from 2008 to 2011. There were a few different displacements on offer, but the most common is the 125cc version. The PX has a classic steel body, a 4-speed manual transmission and a 9-horse power 2-stroke at a good 56 MPH (90 KPH). My PX125 (or P125X) is a 30th anniversary model and was previously tax exempt in London’s Ultra Low Emission Zone (ULEZ). Technically, only motorcycles with a Euro 3 emissions classification and above are exempt, but some Euro 2 models, like my PX, were also exempt due to low NOx emissions.
